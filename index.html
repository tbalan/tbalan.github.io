Biography

I am a PhD student in the department of Medical Statistics and Bioinformatics at Leiden University Medical Center. My work is centered around complex time-to-event data, mostly in the field of random effect (frailty models) in survival analysis. In my spare time, I like to put my skills to use for good causes. I am the maintainer the R package frailtyEM, which I co-wrote with Hein Putter.

Software

frailtyEM is an R package that may be used for fitting shared frailty models. For a Breslow-type non-parametric baseline hazard, several frailty distributions are supported: gamma, inverse Gaussian, positive stable, PVF-family. Both clustered failures and recurrent events are supported, and prediction curves are easily obtained.

The stable version is on CRAN and development version on GitHub. For a more detailed presentation of models and methods, as well as an overview of the implemented estimation approach, I wrote a comprehensive vignette for the package.

The package is still under development, so any feedback is more than welcome.

Publications

Ascertainment correction in frailty models for recurrent events data

Selection bias is a widespread issue in observational studies. It turns out that it may be particularly subtle when working with recurrent events from registry data. In this paper, a general method to correct for this is explored.
TA Balan, MA Jonker, PC Johannesma, H Putter
Statistics in Medicine (2016)

Score test for association between recurrent events and a terminal event

When you are modeling a recurrent event process, death or any other terminal event may be a form of dependent censoring. This paper looks into ways of testing whether the terminal event is associated with the recurrent event process.
TA Balan, SE Boonk, MH Vermeer, H Putter
